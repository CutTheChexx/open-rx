# Copy Craft — Words That Work

Good code with bad copy is a good product nobody understands. Fix the words.

## The Golden Rules

1. **Clear beats clever** — If they have to think about what it means, rewrite it
2. **Talk like a human** — Not a corporation, not a robot, not a marketer. A helpful human
3. **Front-load the value** — Lead with what they GET, not what you DO
4. **One CTA per screen** — If everything is a call to action, nothing is
5. **Error messages should help, not blame** — "Password must be 8+ characters" not "Invalid password"
6. **Empty states are opportunities** — Don't just say "no data." Guide them to create it
7. **Kill your adjectives** — "Fast, reliable, powerful platform" means nothing. Be specific

## Headlines

### The Formula That Works
```
[Action verb] + [specific outcome] + [in timeframe / without pain point]

Examples:
"Track every lead without touching a spreadsheet"
"Build dashboards in minutes, not weeks"
"Score opportunities automatically so you bid on winners"
"Send proposals that close — in half the time"
```

### Hero Section Copy
```
BAD:
"Welcome to Our Innovative Platform"
"The Ultimate Solution for Modern Businesses"
"Empowering Teams with AI-Driven Insights"

GOOD:
"Your leads are scattered. This fixes that."
"Stop checking 5 websites. Check one dashboard."
"Know which deals to chase — and which to drop."
```

### Headline Formulas
```
The "So you can" formula:
  [What it does] so you can [what they want]
  "Automates bid tracking so you can focus on winning work"

The "Without" formula:
  [Desired outcome] without [the pain]
  "Grow your pipeline without cold calling"

The "Stop / Start" formula:
  "Stop guessing which leads are hot. Start knowing."

The Question formula:
  "Spending 2 hours a day on manual data entry?"
  (Only works when you KNOW the answer is yes)
```

## CTAs (Calls to Action)

### Button Copy Rules
```
DO:
  "Start free trial"       — specific action + what they get
  "See my dashboard"       — personal ("my"), specific destination
  "Get the report"         — specific deliverable
  "Book a 15-min call"     — specific time commitment (lowers friction)

DON'T:
  "Submit"                 — submit what? to whom?
  "Click here"             — meaningless
  "Learn more"             — lazy (use only if truly secondary)
  "Get started"            — too vague for primary CTA
```

### CTA Hierarchy
```
Primary CTA (one per page):
  High contrast button, action-oriented
  "Start tracking leads — free"

Secondary CTA:
  Lower contrast, text link or ghost button
  "See how it works →"

Tertiary CTA:
  Plain text link
  "Compare plans"
```

## Microcopy Patterns

### Form Labels & Placeholders
```
BAD:
  Label: "Email"
  Placeholder: "Enter your email"  ← redundant

GOOD:
  Label: "Work email"
  Placeholder: "you@company.com"   ← shows format
  Helper: "We'll send your login link here"  ← explains why
```

### Validation Messages
```
BAD:                          GOOD:
"Invalid email"               "That doesn't look like an email — check for typos"
"Required field"              "We need your name to set up your account"
"Password too short"          "Password needs at least 8 characters"
"Invalid format"              "Phone number should look like (555) 123-4567"
```

### Loading States
```
BAD:                          GOOD:
"Loading..."                  "Pulling your latest leads..."
"Please wait"                 "Crunching the numbers..."
"Processing"                  "Saving your changes..."
                              "Almost there — scoring 47 opportunities..."
```

### Empty States
```
BAD:
"No data found"
"Nothing here"
"0 results"

GOOD:
[Icon or illustration]
"No leads yet"
"Add your first lead and watch your pipeline come alive."
[+ Add a Lead] button

BETTER:
[Icon]
"Your dashboard is ready — it just needs data"
"Import a CSV or add leads manually to get started."
[Import CSV]  [+ Add Lead]
```

### Confirmation Dialogs
```
BAD:
"Are you sure?"
[OK] [Cancel]

GOOD:
"Delete 'Acme Corp' lead?"
"This removes the lead and all its activity history. You can't undo this."
[Keep Lead]  [Delete Lead]

Key: Name the thing. Explain the consequence. Label buttons with actions, not OK/Cancel.
```

### Success Messages
```
BAD:                          GOOD:
"Success!"                    "Lead saved — it's now in your pipeline"
"Done"                        "Email sent to 3 contacts"
"Record updated"              "Changes saved. Your team can see them now."
```

### Tooltips & Help Text
```
Rule: If you need a tooltip, the label might be unclear.
Fix the label first. Tooltip is a backup.

GOOD tooltip use:
  [Score (?)]
  Tooltip: "0-100 based on service match, budget fit, and timeline"

BAD tooltip use:
  [Submit (?)]
  Tooltip: "Click to submit the form"  ← obvious, useless
```

## Landing Page Copy Structure

### Above the Fold
```
1. HEADLINE — What it does + who it's for (8-12 words max)
2. SUBHEADLINE — How it works or the key benefit (15-25 words)
3. CTA — One clear action button
4. SOCIAL PROOF — "Trusted by 500+ teams" or logos

Example:
  H1: "Your sales pipeline, finally in one place"
  Sub: "Track leads, score opportunities, and close faster —
        without the spreadsheet chaos."
  CTA: [Start free — no credit card]
  Proof: "Used by 2,000+ sales teams"
```

### Below the Fold (Scroll Sections)
```
1. PROBLEM AGITATION — "You're doing it the hard way"
2. SOLUTION — "Here's what changes"
3. FEATURES (3-5 max) — Each with icon, heading, 1-sentence description
4. SOCIAL PROOF — Testimonials, case studies, logos
5. PRICING (if applicable) — Clear plans, highlight recommended
6. FAQ — Address objections disguised as questions
7. FINAL CTA — Same as hero CTA, repeated
```

### Feature Descriptions
```
BAD:
"Real-Time Data Synchronization"
"Our platform leverages cutting-edge technology to synchronize
your data in real-time across all devices."

GOOD:
"See changes instantly"
"When your team updates a lead, everyone sees it immediately.
No refreshing. No waiting."
```

## Email Copy Patterns

### Subject Lines
```
DO:
  "Your pipeline report is ready"        — specific, expected
  "3 leads went cold this week"          — specific number, urgency
  "Quick question about your project"    — personal, short
  "[Name], your trial ends Friday"       — personal, deadline

DON'T:
  "Important update!!"                   — vague, spammy
  "You won't believe what happened"      — clickbait
  "Newsletter #47"                       — boring, no reason to open
  "RE: RE: RE: Follow up"               — deceptive
```

### Email Body Framework (Problem → Agitate → Solve)
```
Line 1: Hook — acknowledge their world
  "Running a team means tracking a lot of moving parts."

Line 2-3: Agitate — name the specific pain
  "And when leads slip through the cracks because they're in three
   different spreadsheets, that's revenue walking out the door."

Line 4-5: Solve — show the better way
  "That's why we built [thing]. One dashboard, every lead, scored
   and ranked so you always know what to work on first."

Line 6: CTA — one action
  "See it in action → [link]"

Line 7: Sign off — human, not corporate
  "— Alex"
```

## Voice & Tone Spectrum

```
CASUAL (chat, tooltips, empty states):
  "Nice — your lead just moved to 'Closed Won' 🎯"

PROFESSIONAL (emails, documentation, pricing pages):
  "Your subscription has been updated to the Pro plan.
   Changes take effect immediately."

URGENT (errors, warnings, security):
  "Your session has expired. Log in again to continue.
   Your work has been saved."

EMPATHETIC (failures, cancellations, bad news):
  "We weren't able to process your payment.
   Check your card details and try again — your data is safe."
```

## Anti-Patterns

- **Don't use "we" more than "you"** — Flip every sentence to be about THEM, not you
- **Don't use jargon without context** — "Webhook" means nothing to a non-developer. Say "automatic notification"
- **Don't write paragraphs in UI** — If it's more than 2 sentences in a tooltip or dialog, cut it
- **Don't use passive voice in CTAs** — "Your report can be downloaded" → "Download your report"
- **Don't fear whitespace** — Let the copy breathe. Dense text walls don't get read
- **Don't copy competitors' copy** — Their audience isn't your audience. Write for YOUR people
